What it monitors

Six dimensions of authenticity

Keystamp builds a behavioral fingerprint of the writing process — not the content — to determine if work was genuinely authored by a human.

⌨️

Keystroke Dynamics

Analyzes typing rhythm, speed patterns, and inter-key timing. Every person types differently — like a fingerprint for writing.

🪟

Window Monitoring

Tracks which applications are in focus during writing. Detects if work was composed elsewhere and pasted in.

📋

Clipboard Analysis

Monitors copy-paste events and content size. Distinguishes between normal referencing and bulk content insertion.

⚙️

Process Monitoring

Observes running applications during sessions. Flags if AI tools or unauthorized apps are launched alongside the editor.

📊

Trust Scoring

Combines all behavioral signals into a single trust score with a letter grade. Easy for professors to interpret at a glance.

📄

Tamper-Proof Reports

Generates PDF reports with SHA-256 integrity hashing. Every report is cryptographically verified and cannot be altered.

How it works

Simple for students.
Clear for professors.

Keystamp runs quietly in the background while students write. When they're done, the evidence speaks for itself.

01

Student opens Keystamp

Before starting an assignment, the student launches Keystamp and begins a new session. It takes two seconds.

02

Write naturally

Keystamp monitors typing patterns, window focus, and workflow in the background. Students just write as they normally would — no behavior changes needed.

03

End the session

When finished, the student ends the session. Keystamp calculates a trust score based on all collected behavioral data.

04

Submit the report

A tamper-proof PDF report is generated with the trust score, behavioral breakdown, and SHA-256 integrity hash. Students submit it alongside their work.
